Sharper Leaders

An Evidence-Based Review of the LQ8 Leadership Framework

A redesigned publication aligned to the Leadership Quarter site visual system: capability-first, evidence-led, and practical for executive decision-making.

Author: Jason Hunt • Updated Design Edition • September 2025

Executive Summary

The LQ8 framework defines modern leadership as a learnable system of eight competencies, organised across four connected quadrants. The core finding is that outcomes improve most when organisations treat these competencies as interdependent, not isolated skills.

Inner Compass

Emotional Intelligence and Ethical Leadership establish trust, credibility, and behavioural consistency.

Thinking in Motion

Strategic Thinking and Adaptability improve direction-setting under complexity and uncertainty.

Relating to Others

Collaboration and Stakeholder Influence convert intent into coordinated, cross-boundary execution.

Progress & Growth

Execution & Accountability and Learning Agility sustain delivery and continuous capability uplift.

Method and Position

The review synthesises peer-reviewed leadership, organisational psychology, and performance literature. Evidence supports each competency independently and, more importantly, confirms system-level effects when the competencies are developed in combination.

  • Leadership effectiveness is better explained by capability systems than trait-only models.
  • Trust and psychological safety act as force multipliers for execution and influence.
  • Learning agility functions as a meta-competency that accelerates improvement across all others.

Quadrant 1

Inner Compass

Emotional Intelligence

Supports composure, conflict quality, and relational trust. Strong links to engagement and team cohesion when paired with candour and accountability.

Ethical Leadership

Builds integrity, fairness, and transparency in decision environments. Associated with higher trust, stronger commitment, and lower misconduct risk.

Quadrant 2

Thinking in Motion

Strategic Thinking

Prioritises long-term value under uncertainty; improves alignment between daily execution and strategic goals.

Adaptability

Enables recalibration under change without losing directional intent. A key differentiator in volatility and one of the strongest derailment risk mitigators.

Quadrant 3

Relating to Others

Collaboration

Creates cross-functional coherence, faster issue resolution, and better implementation reliability.

Stakeholder Influence

Mobilises support through credibility, coalition-building, and aligned trade-off decisions.

Quadrant 4

Progress & Growth

Execution & Accountability

Converts ambition into measurable delivery through clear goals, ownership discipline, and feedback loops.

Learning Agility

Raises adaptation speed and transfer learning quality. Especially predictive of leadership potential in new or ambiguous contexts.

System Synthesis

Why the Model Works as a System

The framework’s practical advantage is interaction effect. For example, execution strength without trust can produce compliance but not commitment; influence without ethical grounding can generate short-term motion with long-term fragility.

  • Foundation logic: Inner Compass enables reliable collaboration and influence.
  • Decision logic: Strategic Thinking + Adaptability balances direction and recalibration.
  • Delivery logic: Execution quality depends on relational and ethical context.
  • Acceleration logic: Learning Agility compounds all other competency gains.

Implementation Guidance

How to Use LQ8 in Practice

Assess Holistically

Diagnose patterns across competencies rather than optimising a single score.

Target Root Causes

Address upstream trust, ethics, and clarity drivers before downstream performance symptoms.

Build Operating Cadence

Use stretch assignments, reviews, and decision debriefs to convert insight into behavioural shift.

Track Outcomes

Tie capability changes to engagement, execution reliability, retention, and performance metrics.

Conclusion

LQ8 offers a practical leadership architecture for environments where complexity, pace, and stakeholder scrutiny are increasing. The strongest evidence-backed use case is not isolated competency training, but integrated capability development tied directly to decision quality and execution outcomes.
